Fak'ugesi Awards

Celebrating the Best
in African Digital Creativity

The Fak'ugesi Awards are the premier recognition of digital creative excellence on the African continent.
Presented annually as a highlight of the Fak'ugesi African Digital Innovation Festival, the Awards honour outstanding creative work across immersive media, video games, animation, digital art, design and music. Now in their fourth edition, the Awards have become the definitive measure of where African digital creativity is and where it is headed.
Founded in 2023 as a collaboration between Tshimologong Digital Innovation Precinct and the Wits School of Arts Digital Arts Department, the Fak'ugesi Awards exist on a simple conviction: the most significant digital creative work on the continent deserves to be seen, named and celebrated.

Fak'ugesi 2025

Each category recognises projects that expand the field, work that challenges inherited assumptions, opens new creative territory, or reframes how technology is used within African contexts.

The Awards are not a retrospective exercise. They are a live record of the active fields they recognise. Applications and nominations are open to completed projects, prototypes, and early-stage work. The core criterion is not the state of completion but a compelling minimum viable product.

The Awards are adjudicated by a panel drawn from creative technology, academia, design, media, and industry leadership. To be shortlisted is to enter serious conversation with some of the most consequential digital work being produced on the continent today. The Awards do not reward proximity to a global standard. They reward the work that is building the next one.

The Festival Director's Award is being inaugurated at Fak'ugesi 2026 to recognise practitioners whose work is fundamentally expanding the possibilities of African digital culture, honouring imagination sustained over time and the longer arc of influence.

Requirements

  1. 1. Applicants must be 18 or older and reside in any African country. Submitted work must be the applicant's own original creation.
  2. 2. Applications must be submitted in one category only. Prototypes and early-stage work are eligible alongside completed projects. The core criterion is a compelling minimum viable product.
  3. 3. Work involving jointly owned intellectual property must include written consent from all rights holders. Self-nominations and third-party nominations are both accepted.
  4. 4. Nominations open August 2026. The adjudication panel is drawn from creative technology, academia, design, media, and industry leadership. Panel members are announced when nominations open.
