The Tshimologong Digital Innovation Precinct is a purpose-built environment adjacent to the campus of Wits University in Johannesburg. Designed to accelerate the conditions under which African digital innovation occurs, scales, and circulates, the Precinct houses research groups, start-up incubators, creative studios, and industry partnerships inside a shared innovation environment.

Its mandate is practical: reduce the distance between discovery and deployment, between the laboratory and the market, between the individual practitioner and the structural support they need to build something that lasts.

During Fak'ugesi, the Precinct becomes the physical centre of gravity for the festival's five days. A place where the African Imaginaries are not only discussed but built, tested, exhibited, and released into the world.

Opened as Africa's first full-sized planetarium, the Wits Anglo American Digital Dome has been reimagined as a state-of-the-art immersive venue for digital storytelling, research and innovation. Its 360° projection environment makes it the only one of its kind on the continent and a distinctive space for exploring the intersection of art, science and technology, hosting everything from fulldome cinema and live performance to experimental digital works.

Located at the heart of the University of the Witwatersrand in Braamfontein, the Origins Centre is one of Africa's leading museums dedicated to the story of humankind. Through world-renowned rock art, archaeology, fossils, contemporary installations and immersive exhibitions, it celebrates Africa as the birthplace of humanity, creativity and innovation. As the home of Fak'ugesi's opening programme, the Origins Centre provides a powerful setting where deep history meets bold digital futures.

Johannesburg's only purpose-built live music venue designed with modern acoustic engineering, offering an intimate setting for exceptional performances across jazz, classical, contemporary and experimental music. Located in the Wits cultural precinct between the Origins Centre and Wits Art Museum, the award-winning venue blends striking contemporary architecture with a restored heritage foyer, making it one of the city's premier spaces for music, conversation and creative exchange.

Home to one of the most significant collections of African art on the continent, the Wits Art Museum sits in the heart of the Braamfontein cultural precinct. Its galleries house historical, modern and contemporary works spanning painting, sculpture, textiles and digital media, offering a rich counterpoint to the Festival's exploration of African futures.
