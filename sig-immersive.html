Immersive Africa

The programme presents fulldome immersions, extended-reality works, and spatial sound environments developed by creatives from across the continent. Works include both originals conceived specifically for the Dome's architecture and adaptations that find new form inside it. Each selection rests on a single principle: by Africans, for Africans, and through an African lens. Global best practice and global practitioners are incorporated and showcased.

Kancícà

Kancícà (meaning "link" in Fongbe) is an immersive art and film experience that traces the journey of Na Agontimé, queen-mother of Danxomè's Kingdom, and the living cultural heritage she carried across the Atlantic through generations.

Kwasukasukela

Kwasukasukela, meaning “In the Beginning” in isiZulu, is an immersive projection installation by South African poet Xabiso Vili and Tshimologong Alumni, as well as interdisciplinary artist and director, Inka Kendzia.

This 360° experience fuses poetry, animation, AI, and African mythology to reimagine creation stories within a sangoma’s hut, inviting audiences into a transformative space of healing and ancestral connection. It was produced by a French partner and originally commissioned for the inaugural Forum Création Africa in Paris, with funding from the French.
Ministry for Europe and Foreign Affairs and the support of the French Institute of South Africa, and produced by Dream Feel Factory and Maison des Mondes Africans.

Fulldome projection is an immersive projection format designed for dome-shaped architecture. A fulldome environment surrounds the viewer 360 degrees. The Wits Anglo American Digital Dome uses an 8K projection system, making it the most technically advanced public storytelling infrastructure on the African continent.
The expo is where work is encountered: displayed and engaged with at the viewer's own pace. The showcase is a structured programme in which work is experienced sequentially, curated, timed, and fully immersive. Some Immersive Africa works sit in one category; others bridge both. The programme will indicate which format applies.
Commissioning and selection follows a curated process. Both original works and adaptations of existing work are considered. Proposals from African creatives working in fulldome, XR, and spatial sound are welcomed through the Get Involved section.
